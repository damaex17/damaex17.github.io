Rolex Explorer - Ref. 114270

The Rolex Explorer 114270 is a pure sportswatch. It doesn’t have a rotating bezel or a date complication. It’s a very plain and simple watch and could be mistaken for a dress watch. But then it has elements of a field watch with the super legible 3,6 and 9 markers. I consider it the perfect under the radar tool watch.

History

Created in 1953 to honor the adventures of the first successful expedition to the top of Everest by Hillary and Norgay, the Rolex Explorer was the beginning of a long line of Rolex tool watches designed to go anywhere.

The Explorer was developed from the 1930s Oyster Perpetual “bubblebacks” and was the first water-resistant and self-winding wristwatches to achieve technical and commercial success. The continuous improvements by Rolex led to the Explorer 114270.

The Watch

The average person understands that the Rolex brand stands as a synonym for quality. In the Explorer, Rolex delivers. The Geneva watchmaker ranks among the exclusive club of authentic manufacturers that produce virtually every part of their watches by themselves.

The 36mm stainless steel case itself is an excellent example of Rolex’s eye for quality and commitment, made from the 904L type steel produced in the Rolex owned foundry.

904L steel is more expensive to produce and harder to work with but also more resilient than the lesser 316L employed by the majority of watchmakers. The payoff is the high scratch resistance over the watches’ whole lifetime. The steel preserves the polished and brushed finishes, and the corrosion resistance is higher than with conventional stainless steel. Rolex is building watches that last a lifetime (or two, or three).

Inside the case is the Rolex Cal. 3130 chronometer grade movement, which beats at 28.800 vibrations per hour. The movement is COSC certified in a two-week evaluation process. This certification tests the movement in different positions and under varyin temperatures. The Rolex calibre 3130 has a power reserve of 48 hours, and it is fitted with a parachrom hairspring, offering higher resistance to shocks and to temperature variations.

More than any other aspect of the watch, the dial is what makes the Explorer unique.

Three Arabic numerals at 3, 6, and 9 o’clock combined with the triangular marker at 12 and intermediate stick indexes, no date, smooth bezel. The signature cathedral hands are, like all indexes, made from from white gold for enduring resistance to corrosion and tarnish. The rich black dial provides a contrastful backdrop fo the white gold elements.

Specifications

Model Number: Rolex Explorer Ref. 114270

Movement: Rolex Cal. 3130

Case Material: 904L Stainless Steel

Glass Material: Sapphire Crystal

Lug Width: 20mm

Conclusion

The Rolex Explorer Ref. 114270 is the last of the 36mm Explorers. It represents a time where Rolex built tools rather than jewelry and investment pieces. But make no mistake… the Explorer can be worn with a suit to an evening in the opera. If there is one lesson learned from history, it’s that a Rolex Explorer is your companion anywhere.

I bought the 114270 in 2016, and since then, it has become my daily companion. It might just be the perfect Rolex. The Explorer is durable, accurate and doesn’t get in your way. The design is timeless and blends in perfectly. People don’t associate the brand Rolex with this inconspicuous watch. This Ref. 114270 is, for me, the ultimate Oyster Professional model in the history of the company.
